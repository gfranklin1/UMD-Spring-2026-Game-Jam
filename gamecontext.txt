Overall Game
Essentially you are a crew of old 1600s pirates who are ahead of their time. They have rigged up a vintage diving suit, an air pump, and a winch (raise/lower). You sail across the ocean with a treasure map, anchor over dive sites, and send a diver into the deep to loot everything.

However, you have a quota to meet. The ocean gets darker and deeper as you go, your air is finite, and some things down there are out to get you.

Every player is the same pirate. Nobody is locked into a role. Anyone can put on the diving suit, anyone can man the air pump, anyone can steer the ship. The difficult part is choosing, because if everyone dives, nobody is pumping air, and you’ll all suffocate. When the merchant boat pulls up, sell your haul, buy upgrades, and push deeper.
Core Gameplay Loop
The game follows a repeating quota cycle:
Sail - navigate to a dive site through the treasure map
Anchor - drop anchor and deploy the winch at the dive site
Dive - One or more players put on a diving suit and descend
Support - remaining players manage the air pump, winch, and ship
Surface - Haul loot back to the ship
Sell - merchant boat pulls up, where you sell loot and buy upgrades.
This could also work by selling at islands instead
Upgrade - spend money on gear and/or ship improvements
Repeat - until the quota is met or deadline hits
Quota System
Each cycle the crew must meet a value (gold?) quota
Loot has varying gold values, like shells or mini trinkets are cheap, but buried treasure is worth a lot
A merchant boat pulls alongside your ship at the end of each cycle. Sell look directly from your deck to count toward the quota.
If you miss the quota, the game ends. No second chances
After meeting a quota, the next quota is higher, so players must go to deeper or riskier sites
The merchant also sells gear and ship upgrades, so the sell/buy/next-cycle flow happens in one place
Difficulty scales, so deeper dives imply better loot and more threats with less lights and a longer cable needed.
Solo Play
Playing solo involves the same mechanics. There is no special “solo mode”. You’re one pirate doing everything yourself, such as pumping the air, putting on a dive suit, diving to sites, coming back up when oxygen gets low, pump again, etc. It’s much harder because you can’t do both at once (although there could be an upgrade for this). 
Player Info
Each player has the same universal pirate character (maybe different aesthetics, but that's all). There are no specific classes, no role selection screens, and no locked abilities. The role division would come from player choice and the natural consequences of those choices.

Player Controller
First-person pirate who can move freely on the ship deck and interact with any station or equipment.
Ship stations - walk up to the air pump, winch, or ship wheel and interact
Diving suit - a physical item on the ship. Walk up to it, put it on, and you can go over the side into the water. Only one suit exists by default (more could be unlocked as an upgrade?)
Underwater movement - when wearing the suit, movement is slower and heavier. Helmet flashlight can be activated
Loot - pick up items from the ocean floor, open chests, dig with a shovel (if owned)
Inventory - limited carry capacity, upgradeable with bags. Weights would take up inventory slots
Signal - maybe a ping to communicate to the ship from underwater (e.g. ask to be reeled up)?
Ship Stations
The ship would have interactable stations that any player can use. Walking up to a station and pressing interact locks you into it until you walk away (or press interact again?).
Air pump - actively pumps air to the diver(s) below. If nobody is pumping, oxygen drains faster. Could be a rhythmic minigame (timed button presses) or a hold-to-pump mechanic
Winch - controls how much cable is let out. Maybe reeling too fast causes the diver to drop loot?
Ship wheel - steers and sails the ship between dive sites. Simple wind + rudder controls
Diving suit rack - where the diving suit is stored. Interact to put it on and prepare to dive. Only one suit at the start.
Loot storage - crate/chest on deck where surfaced loot is deposited. This is what gets sold to the merchant.

Emergent Roles
In a 2-player game, one might dive while the other pumps air and manages the winch. Or both could dive quickly, knowing their oxygen will drain fast with nobody pumping.
In a 3-4 player game, you might have one diver, one on the pump, one on the winch, and one watching for threats. Or two divers and one topside player juggling both stations.
Solo means doing everything yourself. Much harder.

Merchant Boat
Instead of a dockable island outpost, a merchant trader in a small boat can pull up to your ship at the end of each quota cycle. This would keep the sell/upgrade flow tight and eliminate the need for weird land docking systems or modeling outposts.

How it would work:
At the end of each cycle (or when summoned?), the merchant boat appears and pulls up alongside your ship.
Interact with the merchant directly from your deck - no sailing to a separate location
Sell look from your ship’s storage to count toward the quota
Browse and purchase gear upgrades and ship upgrades from the merchant’s inventory
Once the transaction is done, the merchant sails off and the next cycle begins
Looks:
Shady trader vibes, maybe fun voice lines or unique boat designs (visually distinct)
Gravity?
We could have dive weights that allow you to descend to the sea floor faster. Removing them would make you ascend. There could also be a weight effect to the items you pick up, meaning the more you hold, the more you weigh and slower you move. We could also make it so you need weights to stay grounded under water, otherwise you slowly float up.
